General Roman Calendar – Novus Ordo

About This Calendar

This subscription feed provides the General Roman Calendar as used in the Novus Ordo (the post-Vatican II form of the Roman Rite). It follows the liturgical reforms implemented after the Second Vatican Council (1962-1965), promulgated in the Missale Romanum of 1969 under Pope St. Paul VI.

These reforms simplified the calendar, restored ancient practices, adjusted the ranking of feasts, and placed renewed emphasis on the Paschal Mystery. This is the calendar in use in most Catholic parishes worldwide today.

By subscribing, you can see the Church’s seasons and major feasts—such as Advent, Christmas, Lent, Easter, and celebrations of saints—directly in your own calendar app.
